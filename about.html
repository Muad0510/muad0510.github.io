About Me

I'm a 3D artist who genuinely believes that the best art is invisible — when the craft disappears and only the feeling remains.

Growing up I was equally obsessed with video games and the natural world. That tension — between the synthetic and the organic — still drives most of my work today. I'm drawn to the moments where something digital starts to feel real.

I've been doing this professionally for over 8 years, across games, film, and interactive experiences. I've led art teams, built pipelines from scratch, shipped titles to millions of players, and spent more hours in Blender than I care to admit.

Outside of work, I spend time hiking, collecting 35mm film cameras that I never actually use, and contributing to open-source Blender add-ons. I'm based in Amsterdam, originally from somewhere with worse weather.

How I Work

A few things I believe deeply

🔬

Technical before aesthetic

Beautiful work that breaks the engine isn't beautiful, it's expensive. I care about poly count, UV efficiency, and LODs — not as constraints, but as part of the craft.

👁

Storytelling through environment

Every prop has a history. Every surface has been touched by someone. I think about the fictional people who live in the spaces I build, even if the player never consciously notices.

⚡

Feedback is information

Criticism early is cheap. Criticism in the final week of crunch is catastrophic. I ask for feedback obsessively and early, and I give it the same way — specific, actionable, without ego.

🌿

Reference everything

The fastest way to make something look fake is to rely on memory. My reference folders are embarrassingly large. I take my own reference photos. I study how light actually behaves.
